Reflection on Validation

Errors encountered: During initial validation, I found two errors: a missing alt attribute on a gallery thumbnail image, and an improperly nested <div> inside a <ul> in the footer. Both were corrected before final submission.

How resolved: I added the missing alt text and replaced the nested <div> with a <li> element.

What I learned: Running validation early in the process — not just at the end — would have caught these issues sooner. I now validate after completing each page rather than waiting until all pages are done. The W3C validator is an invaluable tool for catching structural mistakes that browsers silently correct but that affect accessibility and standards compliance.
