Reflection on the Validation Process

Errors encountered

During initial validation of profile.html, the W3C validator flagged two issues:

  • A missing alt attribute on the site logo SVG element — resolved by adding aria-label and role="img".
  • An unclosed <div> in the footer section — resolved by correcting the HTML nesting.

The sitemap.html initially produced a warning about aria-label on the SVG <a> elements; this was resolved by also adding a <title> child element inside each link group, which is the recommended SVG accessibility pattern.

What I learned

Running validation early and frequently is far more efficient than validating at the end. Small structural errors (missing closing tags, duplicate IDs) compound over time and become harder to trace. I also learned that SVG embedded in HTML5 follows slightly different rules from standalone SVG files — in particular, the xmlns attribute is optional but good practice, and xlink:href is deprecated in favour of plain href.

The validation process also prompted me to revisit my accessibility implementation, leading to improvements in ARIA attributes and focus states that I had initially overlooked.
